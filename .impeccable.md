# Impeccable — Design Context

Synthesized from PRODUCT.md and the established design system (`frontend/src/app.css`,
the Claude Design prototype). The visual language is intentional and must be preserved —
do not swap fonts/colors for generic alternatives.

## Design Context

### Users
Authors, worldbuilders, game masters, and editors turning Markdown drafts into polished
fantasy-manuscript PDFs without doing layout by hand. They work with long prose, iterate on
source text and visual assets, and re-export. Calm, focused sessions — the document is the work.

### Brand Personality
Crafted, literary, practical. Medieval-manuscript atmosphere without theatrical props. The
generated book and the bookshelf are the heroes; chrome stays quiet, capable, precise. Three
words: **crafted, literary, calm**.

### Aesthetic Direction
A parchment workshop that becomes a warm reading room. Established system (keep it):
- **Type**: Spectral (chrome/body), Cinzel (display), EB Garamond (manuscript body),
  IM Fell English (scribe). Manuscript faces (`Forge *`) appear only inside previews, covers,
  and the reader — never in chrome.
- **Color**: parchment/cream surfaces, deep ink/sepia text, **oxblood** primary accent,
  **gilt** for selection/highlight, muted wood/leather for shelving. Tokens in `app.css`.
- **Theme**: light (parchment). Reading/immersive surfaces (Reader, shelf backing) go dark
  leather. This is correct for the calm, daytime authoring context.
- **Anti-references**: generic SaaS dashboards, loud fantasy game menus, flat beige fills,
  default browser controls, heavy decorative frames around the actual book, skeuomorphism that
  impedes reading.

### Design Principles
1. Show the manuscript, not just the controls.
2. Make asset choices visual and reversible.
3. Keep chrome readable and quiet; let book/shelf content carry the richness.
4. Separate app chrome from exported/printed manuscript styling.
5. Treat long documents as the default; motion is supportive and skippable (respect
   `prefers-reduced-motion`).
